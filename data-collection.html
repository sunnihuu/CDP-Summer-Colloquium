The Economic Complexity Observatory: An Analytical Tool for Understanding the Dynamics of Economic Development

Author: Alexander James Gaspar Simões & César A. Hidalgo

Year: 2011

Format: Conference paper

Audience: Researchers, economists, policymakers, and data scientists interested in economic development and trade analytics

This paper introduces the Economic Complexity Observatory (ECO), an online platform designed to visualize and analyze global trade data. The ECO employs network science to map the "product space," illustrating how countries' export profiles relate to their economic complexity and potential for future development. The platform serves as a tool for understanding the dynamics of economic development through the lens of trade and production capabilities.

UN Comtrade Plus

Author: United Nations Statistics Division

Year: Launched in 2022

Format: Web-based trade data platform

Audience: Researchers, policymakers, economists, data scientists, institutions analyzing international trade

UN Comtrade Plus is the United Nations' enhanced platform for accessing detailed and harmonized international trade statistics, reported by nearly 200 countries. It offers granular import/export data across products and services, with tools for advanced filtering, visualization, and bulk downloads. This dataset serves as a primary source for platforms like the Observatory of Economic Complexity (OEC), which uses Comtrade (via the CEPII BACI harmonization) to build its country/product visualizations and economic complexity indices. OEC translates this raw trade data into accessible visual narratives and analytical tools for broader audiences.

UN Comtrade – Global Trade Data Platform

Author: United Nations Statistics Division (UNSD)

Year: Launched in 1962; rebranded as ComtradePlus in 2022

Format: Web-based data portal with APIs and bulk download options

Audience: Economists, policymakers, researchers, data analysts, and institutions requiring detailed international trade statistics

UN Comtrade is the United Nations' comprehensive repository of detailed international trade data reported by nearly 200 countries. It provides granular statistics on goods and services imports and exports categorized by product and country, available through user-friendly interfaces and APIs. This dataset serves as one of the primary data sources for the Observatory of Economic Complexity (OEC), which harnesses Comtrade data (often harmonized via the CEPII BACI dataset) to build interactive visualizations and compute economic complexity indices. OEC translates the raw trade data into accessible visuals and analytical tools, making global trade patterns and country-product relationships understandable for a broad audience.

D3.js (Data-Driven Documents)

Author: Mike Bostock and contributors

Year: Released in 2011; actively maintained

Format: Open-source JavaScript library

Audience: Web developers, data scientists, and visualization specialists

D3.js is a powerful JavaScript library for producing dynamic, interactive data visualizations in web browsers using SVG, HTML, and CSS. It enables binding data to the DOM and applying data-driven transformations to generate complex visual representations. The Observatory of Economic Complexity (OEC) uses D3.js as its core visualization engine to create highly interactive, dynamic graphics. D3.js allows OEC to bind complex trade data to visual elements, animate transitions, control scalable SVG graphics, and integrate interactivity like tooltips and drill-down features—turning large global trade datasets into intuitive, user-friendly visual narratives.

Economic Complexity

Author: Center for Collective Learning (CCL)

Year: Ongoing research; foundational work began in the early 2010s

Format: Research-focused web resource

Audience: Researchers, policymakers, and students interested in economic development, machine learning, and data science

The Center for Collective Learning (CCL) is a multidisciplinary research laboratory focused on understanding the growth, accumulation, and value of socially embedded knowledge. Based at the Toulouse School of Economics and Corvinus University of Budapest, CCL pioneers the application of machine learning techniques to economic development questions. Their work in economic complexity involves developing predictive models to understand the evolution of economies, from cities to nations. CCL's research informs sustainable economic development strategies and has led to the creation of numerous public data observatories, including the Observatory of Economic Complexity (OEC), DataViva, and DataUSA.

Economic Complexity: Theory and Applications

Author: César A. Hidalgo

Year: 2021

Format: Peer-reviewed journal article (PDF available)

Audience: Academics, policymakers, and researchers in economics, development studies, and related fields

This comprehensive review, published in Nature Reviews Physics, delves into the field of economic complexity, focusing on two primary areas: the literature on relatedness, which examines the evolution of specialization patterns, and the literature on metrics of economic complexity, which employs dimensionality reduction techniques to create indices of economic sophistication. The paper explores how these metrics are predictive of variations in income, economic growth, emissions, and income inequality. Additionally, it introduces the Economic Complexity Observatory as a tool for understanding the evolution of countries' productive structures and trade relationships.
